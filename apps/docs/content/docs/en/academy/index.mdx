Academy is the guided way to learn Sim. Where the [Documentation](/introduction) is something you search and skim, Academy is something you move through — a sequence of short, video-first modules that build a working mental model of the platform, one resource at a time.

It's built breadth-first: each module gives you a clear, confidence-building introduction to one part of Sim, then connects it back to the rest. You don't need to finish everything to be productive — but by the end you'll understand how the whole workspace fits together.

<Callout type="info">
Academy and the Documentation share the same ontology. Each module maps to a concept page in the docs — Academy teaches it; the docs let you look it up.
</Callout>

## How Sim fits together

Every module is a resource in the workspace, and they all meet at workflows:

- **Workspace** is the system boundary.
- **Chat** is the natural-language control plane.
- **Workflows** are the executable logic.
- **Tables** hold structured state.
- **Knowledge Bases** are searchable memory.
- **Files** are artifacts.
- **Tools, integrations, MCP, and skills** are capabilities.
- **Deployments** are external surfaces.
- **Logs** are how you verify.

**Learning outcome.** The partner understands that a customer workspace is an operational environment, not just a folder of demos.

## Key beats

1. A workspace contains workflows, tables, files, KBs, integrations, credentials, deployments, and logs.
2. A customer workspace should be organized around systems / use cases.
3. Resources should be named and grouped clearly.
4. The customer should understand what the system does, where data lives, what workflows run, what is deployed, and where to inspect logs.
5. Desired pattern: unified folders / resources would make subsystem organization easier.

**Purpose.** Give the learner the macro object.

**Learning outcome.** By the end, the learner can say:

> Sim is a workspace for building AI systems. You can use Mothership to help create and modify systems, and you can inspect the actual workflows and resources that get created.

## What to say

- Sim is a workbench for building AI systems.
- A Sim workspace contains the things your system uses: workflows, tables, files, knowledge bases, integrations and tools, logs, and deployments.
- Mothership helps you operate that workspace in natural language.
- The important idea: Sim systems are not just chat responses. They're persistent systems made of resources you can inspect, run, and improve.

## Suggested order

1. Open on the Sim workspace.
2. State the one-line definition: *Sim is a workbench for building AI systems.*
3. Show Mothership briefly: *This is the natural-language interface for creating and editing things in your workspace.*
4. Show that there are actual resources behind it: *Workflows, tables, files, knowledge bases, logs, and more.*
5. Close with the promise: *In the next few videos, we'll quickly tour the main objects so you know what you're looking at and where to start.*

## What not to do

- Don't compare too much to Zapier / n8n / Clay.
- Don't explain every module yet.
- Don't get philosophical about Mothership.
- Don't talk about blank-chatbox confusion directly to the learner.

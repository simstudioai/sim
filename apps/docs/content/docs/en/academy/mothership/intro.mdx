**Purpose.** Teach what Mothership is and what role it plays.

**Learning outcome.** The learner understands Mothership as a natural-language builder and control plane — not just a chatbot.

## What to say

Teach Mothership as:

- a natural-language interface over the workspace
- something that can create workflows and resources
- something that can edit and refactor systems
- something that can help inspect and debug
- a layer that sits above the primitives, but does not replace understanding them

> Mothership is how you describe the system you want before you manually reason about every block.

## Key beats

- natural-language builder and control plane
- creates workflows and resources
- works over workspace primitives
- not just a chatbot
- not every output will be perfect on the first try

## What Mothership can see and do

Frame this carefully — without over-indexing on internal implementation:

> Mothership can reason over different parts of your workspace and use internal product capabilities to create, inspect, and revise systems.

What the learner needs to know: Mothership operates over workspace resources, knows about the workflows and objects in the workspace, has more local context in scoped workflow-editing (Copilot), and can often inspect logs and help fix issues. It is not omniscient — it still works through the product's available primitives and resource visibility.

## What not to do

- Don't imply Mothership always produces a perfect first build.
- Don't position it as replacing knowledge of the primitives.
- Don't lean on internal-implementation terms (e.g. "subagents") unless that becomes official product language.

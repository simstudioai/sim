## The context problem

The naive way to give a model knowledge is to paste everything into its context. That breaks down fast — too much text, too much cost, too much noise. A knowledge base is the answer: store the corpus once, and at run time pull back only the passages that bear on the question.

## What's inside

A knowledge base is a resource that lives in your workspace — you can have several. Open one and it holds **documents**, plus **connectors** that bring in and keep documents in sync from outside sources. Sim automatically **indexes and embeds** every document into searchable **chunks**, so the contents become queryable by meaning.

## Searching it

Use a Knowledge block with a query and it returns the most relevant chunks, each with a **similarity score** — how closely it matches the meaning of the query. On its own that's retrieval. The power comes one step later: feed those chunks into an Agent block's context, and the model answers from your actual documents — accurate, up to date, and able to **cite** where each fact came from. That's grounding plus retrieval.
